Digital Phenotyping of the “Invisible” Patient: A Computational Analysis of Nociplastic Pain Narratives on Reddit and TikTok

How patients with medically unexplained pain narrate validation, humor, and education across two very different platforms.

Introduction

Nociplastic pain is characterized by altered pain processing without clear evidence of tissue damage or nervous system injury. Many patients experience medical skepticism and dismissal, leading them to seek support through social media.

This study uses digital phenotyping to analyze the lived experiences of nociplastic pain patients on TikTok and Reddit, identifying narratives and coping mechanisms that can help improve clinical communication.

Why It Matters

Patients with “invisible” pain conditions often encounter dismissal in clinical settings
Reddit and TikTok have become informal spaces for validation, education, and coping
Digital phenotyping can surface unmet patient needs that clinical encounters miss
POTS-related content draws disproportionately high engagement on both platforms

Methods

A custom Python script scraped publicly available posts from eight condition-specific subreddits and six TikTok hashtags, filtered by symptomology and community-identity keywords. After data cleaning, the 50 highest-engaging posts per platform were selected (N=100) and analyzed using a mixed-methods approach combining quantitative engagement metrics with a four-code qualitative rubric.

Coding Rubric

Code 1 · Validation — seeking confirmation that symptoms and experiences are real
Code 2 · Education — sharing medical information or explaining the condition
Code 3 · Humor / Meme — using comedy to cope with or normalize symptoms
Code 4 · Symptom Flair / Vlog — day-in-the-life documentation of symptom flares

Discussion

TikTok NarrativesSeeking validation (36%) and humor/coping (34%) dominate; 58% of videos are framed as day-in-the-life.
Reddit NarrativesMedical education leads (64%), alongside validation-seeking (32%); 79% of posts contain educational content.
Engagement — TikTokPOTS-related content drives 68% of top engagement, the highest of any topic studied.
Engagement — RedditPOTS-related content drives 36% of top engagement — again the single highest topic.

Conclusions

Reddit and TikTok serve distinct but complementary roles for patients with nociplastic pain.
Reddit provides a space for validation and peer education; TikTok normalizes experience through humor and day-in-the-life content.
High POTS engagement highlights significant unmet clinical needs. Understanding these digital narratives can support a more patient-centered model that validates the “invisible” aspects of chronic illness.
