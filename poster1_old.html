Introduction

Nociplastic pain is characterized by altered pain processing without clear evidence of tissue damage or nervous system injury. Many patients experience medical skepticism and dismissal, leading them to seek support through social media.

This study utilizes digital phenotyping to analyse the lived experiences of nociplastic pain patients on TikTok and Reddit, identifying narratives and coping mechanisms to improve communication and patient-centered care.

Methods

A custom Python script scraped publicly available posts from:

  • 8 condition-specific subreddits
  • 6 TikTok hashtags

Posts were filtered by symptomology and community-identity keywords. Following data cleaning, the 50 highest-engaging posts per platform were selected (N=100) and subjected to mixed-methods analysis combining:

  • Quantitative engagement metrics
  • Four-code qualitative rubric (Validation, Humor/Meme, Symptom Flair/Vlog, Education)

Conclusion

Reddit and TikTok serve distinct but complementary roles for patients with nociplastic pain

🔵 Reddit provides:

  • ✓ Space for validation
  • ✓ Peer education
  • ✓ Detailed medical discussions

🔴 TikTok provides:

  • ✓ Normalization through humour
  • ✓ Day-in-the-life content
  • ✓ Visual storytelling

High POTS engagement highlights significant unmet clinical needs.
Understanding digital narratives can support a more patient-centered model that validates the "invisible" aspects of chronic illness.
